|X|X|X|

a door that isolates while feeling open

The image of the open door is a very powerful social sign, metaphorically but also physically. Yet, in a shared living situation you often have to close your room door, not because you want to be isolated or not disturbed, but because of things like the heat escaping the room, music that could annoy others or wind coming from an open window. |X|X|X| addresses this discrepancy by offering an intermediated state of being physically closed but visually open.

How people can be helped within their own homes? The current solution to this seems to be ever more technology and intelligent appliances.A theoretical analysis of the smart home revealed however, that humans can almost always also perform these aids and most of the time better than any technology can. The central idea of |X|X|X| is therefore to foster co-living and inter-human support instead of increasing our reliance on technology.

In co-living, the door has a central and symbolic function, as the room-door separates privacy and openness. At the moment however doors are binary as there are only two possible stages: fully open or fully closed. This however doesn’t fully correlate with the more analog and gradual human experience. Extensive prototyping, looking into methods of designing this transition from open to closed, finally led to origami. Further tests revealed a folding pattern, that allowed to beautifully transition from open to half-open to fully closed.

|X|X|X| is a sliding door that has multiple states: When pulled shut, the door isolates the room with its glass panes. This means no heat, sound or wind can escape the room, while it still lets through most of the light. Or in other words, the door is physically closed but visually open, keeping the connection to the rest of the flat. If the user wishes for more privacy, the door can be pulled shut even further making it fully opaque. The outer section folds away and makes room for the other two elements to unfold completely.

The doors minimalistic construction was conceived through iterative testing and consists of six slender pains of glass connected by four long pieces of stiffened fabric. This fabric acts as the doors hinge, while at the same time giving it its characteristic property of multi staged privacy. When it is pulled completely shut the outer section folds flat, giving the other two sections space to unfold completely and therefore becoming opaque. Because of its minimalistic construction the door could be easily retrofitted into any home.
