paravent

adaptive guidance in public buildings

Paravent is designed for public spaces like big train stations, where adaptive crowd guiding is as important as the right spatial atmosphere. The human sized panels are able to rotate, allowing the space to be adapted to the changing demands through out the day.

– Project realised together with fellow student Luke Handon –

This project was initiated by the DB (Germanys national railway company), when they asked for new solutions for Frankfurt central station. On-site surveys at Frankfurts central station revealed, what they were doing in different parts of the station and how they felt about the different areas. Later we analysed the results and categorised them by topic.

These on-site surveys, combined with further research revealed, that a train station is a very dynamic place with changing needs. Even within a few hundred meters, the local crowds had significant differences in their needs. Rush hour in the morning and evening demands a highly efficient organisation, while during the day most people need a calm, pleasant space to wait for long distance trains. This dynamic change is of course not only true for train stations, but easily translates to other public spaces.

Functional public spaces, like train stations, have two main components: efficient crowd guiding as wells as pleasantly calm spots for waiting etc. To create these calm areas the so-called niche-effect can be utilised, which states, that humans dislike waiting in open spaces but rather tend to look for niches, when waiting or sitting down. To enable guidance as well as the formation of niches we designed a modular, adaptive wall element, that could easily be integrated into existing architecture.

The modular Wall element, consists of an opaque glass pane, pivoted in its frame, so that it can turn around its vertical axis. Several modules are interlocked side by side, creating a dynamic wall, which can then be controlled remotely. This allows intuitive guiding, niche construction and adaptive emergency escape routes, all with one module.
